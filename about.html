About

A friggin' sweet orchestra

Thames Youth Orchestra was founded in 2005 and is based in South West London.

TYO now has a large permanent staff of highly-respected professional musicians and comprises more than eighty young musicians drawn from numerous local schools. Its ethos is defined by a challenging, adventurous approach to programming, which combines well-established large-scale orchestral favourites with twentieth century rarities and new commissions.

TYO has featured in several BBC television programmes, has performed at London's Barbican Centre, Royal Albert Hall, Cadogan Hall, St John's Smith Square, and has played to full houses on tour in venues across Europe.

The Thames Youth Sinfonia is TYO's training orchestra, aimed at players Grades 4 and above. The orchestra's sister ensemble, the Thames Youth Jazz Orchestra, is a big band taking players Grades 7 and above.

TYO rehearses in Kingston upon Thames on Mondays from 6.15 - 8.45pm during term time; the Sinfonia rehearses from 5.00 - 6.00pm on Mondays, and TYJO rehearses 5.30 - 7.00pm on Fridays.
